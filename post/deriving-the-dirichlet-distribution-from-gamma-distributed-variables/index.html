Deriving the Dirichlet Distribution from Gamma Distributed Variables

Last updated on May 31, 2020 5 min read Bayesian Nonparametrics

This post is a continuation of the post that constructs a Beta distribution from Gamma distributed variables. We use the same parameterization of the Gamma Distribution as before, and set the rate parameter \(=1\) unless otherwise specified. In this post I prove that the following property holds:

Given \(Z_i \sim Gamma(\alpha_i,1)\), are IID, (\(\alpha_i > 0\) and \(1 \le i \le n\)) and I define the variables \(U_i = \frac{Z_i}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N} Z_i}\) and \(V = \sum\limits_{i=1}^{N} Z_i\). Then:

  • \((U_1, U_2, \dots, U_N) \sim Dir(\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \dots, \alpha_N) = \frac{\Gamma(\sum \alpha_i)}{\Gamma(\alpha_1)\dots \Gamma(\alpha_N)} u_1^{\alpha_1-1}u_2^{\alpha_1-1}\dots u_N^{\alpha_N-1} = \frac{\Gamma(\sum \alpha_i)}{\Gamma(\alpha_1)\dots \Gamma(\alpha_N)} u_1^{\alpha_1-1}u_2^{\alpha_1-1}\dots (1-\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N-1}u_i)^{\alpha_N-1}\)
  • \(V \sim Gamma(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N} \alpha_i, 1)\)
  • \(U = (U_1, U_2, \dots, U_N)\) and \(V\) are independent

By construction, \(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N} U_i = 1\). The \(N^{th}\) term is redundant (in the sense that it can be obtained from the remaining \((N-1)\) terms). I use the change of variables approach to find the joint distribution of \((U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, V)\). I represent the \(Z_i\)â€™s as functions of \(U_i\) and \(V\) to get:

\[\begin{align} Z_i & = U_i V && 0 \le i \le N-1 \tag{1}\\ Z_N & = (1-\sum_{i=1}^{N-1}U_i)V && \tag{2} \end{align}\]

By definition, the joint density of \((U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, V)\) is given by: \[\begin{align} f_{U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) &= f_{Z_1,\dots,Z_N}(z_1,\dots,z_N) |J_{(z_1,z_2,\dots,z_{N-1},z_N) \rightarrow (u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v)}| & \tag{3} \end{align}\]

The Jacobian matrix is given by: \[\begin{align} J_{(z_1,z_2,\dots,z_{N-1},z_N) \rightarrow (u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v)} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial z_1}{\partial u_1} & \frac{\partial z_1}{\partial u_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial z_1}{\partial u_{N-1}} & \frac{\partial z_1}{\partial v} \\ \frac{\partial z_2}{\partial u_1} & \frac{\partial z_2}{\partial u_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial z_2}{\partial u_{N-1}} & \frac{\partial z_2}{\partial v} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial z_{N-1}}{\partial u_1} & \frac{\partial z_{N-1}}{\partial u_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial z_{N-1}}{\partial u_{N-1}} & \frac{\partial z_{N-1}}{\partial v} \\ \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial u_1} & \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial u_2} & \dots & \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial u_N} & \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial v} \end{bmatrix} \tag{4} \end{align}\]

Given (1) & (2), the partial derivatives in the Jacobian matrix are given by

\[\begin{alignat}{2} \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial u_i} & = v && \quad 0 \le i \le N-1 \tag{5}\\ \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial u_j} & = 0 && \quad j \neq i, 0 \le j \le N-1 \tag{6}\\ \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial v} & = u_i && \quad 0 \le i \le N-1 \tag{7} \\ \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial u_i} & = -v && \quad 0 \le i \le N-1 \tag{8} \\ \frac{\partial z_N}{\partial v} & = 1-\sum_{i=1}^{N-1} u_i \tag{9} \end{alignat}\]

Substituting (5), (6), (7), (8) & (9) in (4), we get \[\begin{align} J_{(z_1,z_2,\dots,z_{N-1},z_N) \rightarrow (u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v)} = \begin{bmatrix} v & 0 & \dots & 0 & u_1 \\ 0 & v & \dots & 0 & u_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \dots & v & u_{N-1} \\ -v & -v & \dots & -v & 1-\sum_{i=1}^{N-1} u_i \end{bmatrix} \tag{10} \end{align}\]

I need the determinant of the Jacobian matrix above. This can be computed easily after making a row transformation operation. Apply the transformation \(R_N \rightarrow R_N + \sum\limits_{i=1}^{N-1} R_i\) (where \(R_i\) is the \(i^{th}\) row of the determinant) to get:

\[\begin{align} |J_{(z_1,z_2,\dots,z_{N-1},z_N) \rightarrow (u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v)}| = \begin{vmatrix} v & 0 & \dots & 0 & u_1 \\ 0 & v & \dots & 0 & u_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \dots & v & u_{N-1} \\ 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = v^{N-1} \tag{11} \end{align}\]

Using result (11) in (3), I get:

\[\begin{align} f_{U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,u_2,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) = f_{Z_1,Z_2,\dots,Z_{N-1},Z_N}(z_1,z_2,\dots,z_{N-1},z_N) v^{N-1} \tag{12} \end{align}\]

Since \(Z_i\) are IID \(Gamma(\alpha_i,1)\) distributed: \[\begin{align} f_{Z_1,\dots,Z_N}(z_1,\dots,z_N) = \prod_{i=1}^{N}f_{Z_i}(z_i) = \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} z_i^{\alpha_i - 1} e^{-z_i} = \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} z_i^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] e^{-\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}z_i} \tag{13} \end{align}\]

Substituting (13) into (12), and using the relations in (1) & (2): \[\begin{align} f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) = & \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} z_i^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] e^{-\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}z_i} \cdot v^{N-1} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) = & \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N-1} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} z_i^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_N)} z_N^{\alpha_N - 1} e^{-v} v^{N-1} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) = & \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N-1} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i v)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_N)} (v(1-\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N-1}u_i))^{\alpha_N - 1} e^{-v} v^{N-1} & \tag{14} \end{align}\]

Since \(U_N = 1 - \sum\limits_{i=1}^{N-1}U_i\), I put this in (14) to get \[\begin{align} f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N-1} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i v)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_N)} (u_N v)^{\alpha_N - 1} e^{-v} v^{N-1} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i v)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] e^{-v} v^{N-1} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] v^{\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i-N \right)} e^{-v} v^{N-1} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] v^{\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i-1 \right)} e^{-v} & \nonumber \end{align}\]

\[\begin{align} \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \Gamma \left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i \right) \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha_i)} (u_i)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] \frac{1}{\Gamma \left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i \right)}v^{\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i-1 \right)} e^{-v} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = \frac{\Gamma \left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i \right)}{\prod\limits_{i=1}^{N} \Gamma(\alpha_i)} \left[ \prod_{i=1}^{N} (u_i)^{\alpha_i - 1} \right] \frac{1}{\Gamma \left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i \right)}v^{\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i-1 \right)} e^{-v} & \nonumber \\ \implies f_{U_1,\dots,U_{N-1}, V}(u_1,\dots, u_{N-1}, v) & = Dir(\alpha_1,\alpha_2, \dots, \alpha_N) \cdot Gamma(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i, 1) & \end{align}\] Which establishes the following:

  1. \((U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, U_{N}) \sim Dir(\alpha_1,\alpha_2, \dots, \alpha_N)\)
  2. \(V \sim Gamma(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}\alpha_i,1)\)
  3. \(U = (U_1,U_2,\dots,U_{N-1}, U_{N})\) & \(V\) are independent

This completes the proof.
